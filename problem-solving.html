# My experience with Problem Solving:

KATA has been tough. I have struggled with understanding the 'why's' of Jvascript methods. I have alot of information that I have absorbed but I found myself struggle to make sense of it, to connect the dots, to make it 'click' before the flow state begins. I felt a lack of motivation to try, but I dealt with that by completing everything I found easy, and making a checklist. Once I ticked off the easy stuff, I felt better about taking time with the technical stuff that I struggled with. Now, I can look at my list and go- "Cool, all that is left is just these 5 tasks" and even if those tasks are very big concepts, when you read 5, you think, oh wow that's all?!

I want to say that moving throught the KATA exercises was as fun and easy as the html and ccss ones, but I got stuck in most of the KATA exercises and had to google the question or ask a friend, and I tried my best to make sense of the explanations I found and I keep revising it so I don't forget it.

My learning around this is practise more, research and revisit the console.log, loops and arrays, objects cause that way it'll settle into my mind better having done these exercises.

Study > Practise > Revisit study

# Problem solving techniques I used, in their order:

- Trying something
I do trial and error for literally everything. I brainstorm and try everything possible, even if it doesn't make sense, it's important, because I know it didn't work and to try a different way next time. You gotta feed the curiosity!

- Googling
My best virtual friend.

- Reading error messages
Lived on this to proceed through each task. Infact, this was my checklist/ questionaire more than the actual question.

- Asking your peers for help
I reached out to my school friends, turns out half of them are coders and they LOVE explaining. And since we grew up together, they understand how I learn and my questions, and there's zero judgement which is really helpful while learning!

- The rubber ducky method
I used this more in the beginning exercises on my own, and then did it with my friends. I did it with listening and looping.

- Console.logging


- Asking coaches for help
Alot of times I got my answers in the time I waited for the coaches to get back to me. Which was a good learning for me, however, a little frustrating too. I did channel the frustration into other tasks and more extensive google search, so there's a positive side to that!:)

- Pseudocode
I didn't do as much of this as I'd like to have, but I am sure it will come in use further along on the coding journey.

- Improving your process with reflection
I think this helps. Writing. These blogs help to see how far I've come and give a sense of clarity cause I have to put words to my thoughts and learnings. It's great!
